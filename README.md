### Project Summary
Complete SQL analysis of patient attendance patterns and billing performance 
using a two-table relational database connecting patient records to billing data.

### Key Findings

**Finding 1 — No-Show Rate is Critically High**  
Four out of ten scheduled patients did not attend their appointments representing 
a 40% no-show rate. This is significantly above the industry benchmark of 5 to 30% 
for primary care clinics. At $660 in billed revenue attached to no-show appointments 
the financial impact is direct and measurable.

**Finding 2 — Provider Performance Disparity**  
Dr. Patel recorded 4 no-shows, average wait time of 17 minutes, outstanding balance 
of $140 and total collected revenue of $720. Dr. Adams recorded zero no-shows, 
average wait time of 11 minutes, outstanding balance of $75 and total collected 
revenue of $955. The disparity across all four metrics requires investigation 
within 30 days.

**Finding 3 — Medicaid Outstanding Balances**  
Medicaid carries the highest outstanding balance at $180 with 4 patients flagged 
for urgent follow up. This pattern typically indicates claim denials due to 
documentation gaps, missed prior authorization or incorrect coding. Appeals should 
be filed within the payer window of 30 to 90 days.

**Finding 4 — Collection Rate**  
Overall clinic collection rate is 88.6%. Private insurance maintained 100% 
collection rate. Medicare and Medicaid are dragging the overall rate down. 
Targeted follow up recommended to push above the 90% benchmark.

### Three Immediate Actions Recommended
1. Implement 48-hour appointment reminders to reduce the 40% no-show rate
2. Review Dr. Patel's scheduling process within 30 days
3. Assign billing specialist to investigate and appeal outstanding Medicaid claims
