Executive Summary
An SQL analysis of patient attendance and billing data for Riverside Primary Care Clinic reveals a 40% no-show rate and an overall collection rate of 88.6%. While collection performance is within acceptable range, provider-level disparities and Medicaid outstanding balances require immediate attention. Total revenue at risk from no-show appointments stands at $660.

Finding 1 — No-Show Rate is Critically High
Four out of ten scheduled patients did not attend their appointments representing a 40% no-show rate. This is significantly above the industry benchmark of 5 to 30% for primary care clinics. At $660 in billed revenue attached to no-show appointments the financial impact is direct and measurable. Implementing 48-hour automated appointment reminders is recommended as an immediate first step.

Finding 2 — Provider Performance Disparity
The analysis reveals a significant performance gap between providers. Dr. Patel recorded 4 no-shows, an average wait time of 17 minutes, an outstanding balance of $140 and total collected revenue of $720. Dr. Adams recorded zero no-shows, an average wait time of 11 minutes, an outstanding balance of $75 and total collected revenue of $955. The disparity across all four metrics suggests a systemic issue with Dr. Patel's scheduling process and patient communication that requires investigation within 30 days.

Finding 3 — Medicaid Outstanding Balances
Medicaid carries the highest outstanding balance at $180 with 4 patients flagged for urgent follow up. Based on US healthcare billing experience this pattern typically indicates claim denials due to documentation gaps, missed prior authorization or incorrect coding. A billing specialist should review each flagged Medicaid claim and file appeals within the payer's appeal window of 30 to 90 days.

Finding 4 — Collection Rate
The overall clinic collection rate of 88.6% is approaching but has not yet reached the 90% benchmark considered healthy for primary care practices. Private insurance patients maintained a 100% collection rate. Medicare and Medicaid are dragging the overall rate down. Targeted follow up on outstanding Medicaid and Medicare balances is recommended to push the overall rate above 90%.

Three Immediate Actions Recommended
One — Implement 48-hour appointment reminders by phone or text for all scheduled patients to reduce the 40% no-show rate.
Two — Conduct a focused review of Dr. Patel's scheduling process and patient communication within 30 days to address the performance gap across all metrics.
Three — Assign a billing specialist to investigate and appeal all outstanding Medicaid claims within the payer appeal window to recover $180 in outstanding balances and prevent future denials.
